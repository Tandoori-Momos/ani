I could see smiles on all the faces as soon as I hit the play button. All the sparkling eyes were ready for what had become a custom in our school to play my mashup of ‘See You Again’ and ‘Kal Ho Na Ho’ as a tribute to the seniors who would graduate in a few months. As all hands raised and started to sway from left to right, we all recollected the fun times we had with our seniors, what they taught us and how much we will miss them. One just can’t stop the tears from rolling down their cheeks when they ponder the fact they may never be able to see them again.

Since grade 9, I have been the DJ for the annual winter carnival of our school. This hobby of mine can be traced all the way back to when I was 11 years old. I stumbled upon some videos of electronic music production and sound design on YouTube. It intrigued me so much that I actually downloaded the necessary software that very day and started with the basics right off the bat.

Before I knew it, my sister and I were rocking to some terrible beats I made with it. It was actually a big deal for me back then that I could get something rhythmic out of the machine, but it was still far from what I wanted to make. I never gave up. With days and nights of tireless hard work, I could hear those dreadful sounds coming close to what could be called ‘music’. Eventually, this hobby of mine became more than just a hobby when I got accepted into the computer club of our school. As I got exposed to various competitions, I ended up bagging several individual and overall prizes for the school. Soon, I started posting my work on SoundCloud: an online platform for independent musicians. My music started getting featured on several YouTube channels and independent labels. I now have over 18k plays on my most successful track.

Now I am preparing for my final performance at this year’s carnival. I have a lot of personal tracks up my sleeve for this set. But when I play that final mashup this year, I know I just won’t be able to stop the tears. It won’t be the seniors that I would be parting from, but my classmates. People with whom I have spent my entire childhood, and who have supported me through every endeavour of mine. I will always relish the memories that we share as they have made me what I am today.
